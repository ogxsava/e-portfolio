Behind the Firewall: What a System Administrator Really Does

Howest IT Student podcast · 28 May · 25 min

I recorded an episode for the Howest IT Student podcast with Eric Symon, who runs his own system administration company. We talked about what the job actually looks like when you are responsible for networks, servers, and uptime — not the textbook version.

What we covered

Eric walked through roughly twenty-eight years in IT and how system administration changed: from simpler network-centric work to today’s mix of cloud, security, automation, and constant availability expectations. We discussed the day-to-day reality behind the firewall — incidents, monitoring, patching, backups, and the judgment calls you make when users only see “the Wi‑Fi is down.”

For me as a student leaning toward security and infrastructure, it was valuable to hear how reliability and communication matter as much as technical depth. Running your own company adds another layer: client trust, scope, and making sure systems you manage actually match how the business works.

Why this conversation

I wanted to go beyond job titles and ask someone in the field how they spend their week — what surprised them early in their career, what they would tell a graduate starting today, and how sysadmin work connects to broader security practice. Eric’s perspective grounded a lot of what I read in class in real maintenance, risk, and longevity of systems.

Reflection

Preparing and hosting the episode pushed me on structure (introduction, main thread, conclusion) and on listening more than talking. Recording with an external professional — not a lecturer — forced clearer questions and follow-ups. I shared the episode on LinkedIn as well; if you work in ops or infrastructure, I would be curious what you would add from your own trenches.
